USMLE Step 1 Practice Questions

Question 1: A 45-year-old woman presents with fatigue, pruritus, and right upper quadrant pain. Physical examination reveals hepatomegaly and jaundice. Laboratory tests show elevated alkaline phosphatase and antimitochondrial antibodies. What is the most likely diagnosis?





Explanation: The most likely diagnosis is Primary biliary cholangitis (B). This is suggested by the patient's presentation of fatigue, pruritus, and jaundice, along with hepatomegaly, elevated alkaline phosphatase, and the presence of antimitochondrial antibodies, which are specific for this condition.

Question 2: A 60-year-old man with a history of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) presents with worsening dyspnea and a productive cough. His temperature is 38.5°C (101.3°F), and he has decreased breath sounds and dullness to percussion over the right lower lung field. A chest X-ray shows consolidation in the right lower lobe. What is the most likely pathogen?





Explanation: The most likely pathogen is Streptococcus pneumoniae (A). This bacterium is the most common cause of community-acquired pneumonia, especially in patients with underlying chronic diseases such as COPD.

Question 3: A 28-year-old woman presents with a severe headache, fever, and nuchal rigidity. A lumbar puncture is performed, and cerebrospinal fluid analysis shows elevated white blood cell count, increased protein, and decreased glucose. What is the most likely diagnosis?





Explanation: The most likely diagnosis is Bacterial meningitis (B). The presence of a severe headache, fever, nuchal rigidity, elevated white blood cell count, increased protein, and decreased glucose in the cerebrospinal fluid are typical findings in bacterial meningitis.

Question 4: A 22-year-old man presents with a painless testicular mass. On ultrasound, the mass is solid and hypoechoic. Serum tumor markers are elevated for alpha-fetoprotein (AFP) and beta-human chorionic gonadotropin (β-hCG). What is the most likely diagnosis?





Explanation: The most likely diagnosis is Yolk sac tumor (C). Elevated levels of alpha-fetoprotein (AFP) and beta-human chorionic gonadotropin (β-hCG) in the presence of a solid, hypoechoic testicular mass suggest a non-seminomatous germ cell tumor, specifically a yolk sac tumor.

Question 5: A 35-year-old woman presents with palpitations, weight loss, and heat intolerance. Physical examination reveals a diffusely enlarged, non-tender thyroid gland. Laboratory tests show elevated free T4 and suppressed TSH. What is the most likely diagnosis?





Explanation: The most likely diagnosis is Graves' disease (B). This condition is characterized by symptoms of hyperthyroidism such as palpitations, weight loss, and heat intolerance, along with a diffusely enlarged thyroid gland, elevated free T4, and suppressed TSH.

Question 6: A 33-year-old woman presents with joint pain, a butterfly-shaped rash on her face, and photosensitivity. Laboratory tests reveal positive antinuclear antibodies (ANA) and anti-double-stranded DNA (anti-dsDNA) antibodies. What is the most likely diagnosis?





Explanation: The most likely diagnosis is Systemic lupus erythematosus (B). The presence of joint pain, a butterfly-shaped rash, photosensitivity, positive ANA, and anti-dsDNA antibodies are characteristic features of systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE).

Question 7: A 40-year-old man presents with epigastric pain that improves with eating. He has a history of chronic NSAID use. Endoscopy reveals a gastric ulcer. What is the most likely underlying cause?





Explanation: The most likely underlying cause is NSAID use (C). Chronic NSAID use can lead to gastric ulcers by inhibiting prostaglandin synthesis, which is protective for the gastric mucosa.

Question 8: A 25-year-old woman presents with a painless, firm thyroid nodule. Fine-needle aspiration biopsy reveals cells with ground-glass nuclei and intranuclear grooves. What is the most likely diagnosis?





Explanation: The most likely diagnosis is Papillary thyroid carcinoma (A). The presence of ground-glass nuclei and intranuclear grooves, also known as Orphan Annie eye nuclei, is characteristic of papillary thyroid carcinoma.

Question 9: A 65-year-old man presents with new-onset back pain, anemia, and renal dysfunction. Serum protein electrophoresis shows an M spike. What is the most likely diagnosis?





Explanation: The most likely diagnosis is Multiple myeloma (A). The combination of back pain, anemia, renal dysfunction, and an M spike on serum protein electrophoresis is indicative of multiple myeloma.

Question 10: A 70-year-old woman presents with progressive shortness of breath, a dry cough, and bilateral crackles on lung auscultation. Pulmonary function tests reveal a restrictive pattern. Chest CT shows honeycombing and reticular opacities predominantly in the lower lobes. What is the most likely diagnosis?





Explanation: The most likely diagnosis is Pulmonary fibrosis (B). The presentation of progressive shortness of breath, dry cough, bilateral crackles, restrictive pattern on pulmonary function tests, and characteristic CT findings of honeycombing and reticular opacities suggest pulmonary fibrosis.

Question 11: A 55-year-old man presents with severe chest pain radiating to his back, which began suddenly. He is diaphoretic and appears anxious. Blood pressure is 150/90 mmHg in the right arm and 120/80 mmHg in the left arm. What is the most likely diagnosis?





Explanation: The most likely diagnosis is Aortic dissection (C). The sudden onset of severe chest pain radiating to the back, along with differential blood pressure in the arms, is highly suggestive of aortic dissection.

Question 12: A 25-year-old woman presents with fever, sore throat, and fatigue. Physical examination reveals cervical lymphadenopathy and splenomegaly. A peripheral blood smear shows atypical lymphocytes. What is the most likely diagnosis?





Explanation: The most likely diagnosis is Infectious mononucleosis (B). The triad of fever, sore throat, and fatigue, along with cervical lymphadenopathy, splenomegaly, and atypical lymphocytes on peripheral blood smear, are characteristic of infectious mononucleosis, commonly caused by Epstein-Barr virus (EBV).

Question 13: A 60-year-old woman presents with progressive difficulty swallowing solids and liquids, weight loss, and regurgitation of undigested food. A barium swallow study shows a "bird beak" appearance of the distal esophagus. What is the most likely diagnosis?





Explanation: The most likely diagnosis is Achalasia (A). The progressive dysphagia to both solids and liquids, weight loss, regurgitation, and "bird beak" appearance on barium swallow study are indicative of achalasia, a motility disorder of the esophagus.

Question 14: A 45-year-old man with a history of chronic alcohol abuse presents with confusion, ataxia, and ophthalmoplegia. What is the most likely underlying cause of his symptoms?





Explanation: The most likely underlying cause is Wernicke's encephalopathy (B). The triad of confusion, ataxia, and ophthalmoplegia is characteristic of Wernicke's encephalopathy, which is caused by a deficiency of thiamine (vitamin B1), often seen in chronic alcohol abusers.

Question 15: A 30-year-old woman presents with heat intolerance, weight loss, and increased sweating. Physical examination reveals a diffusely enlarged thyroid gland and exophthalmos. Laboratory tests show elevated free T4 and suppressed TSH. What is the most likely diagnosis?





Explanation: The most likely diagnosis is Graves' disease (B). The symptoms of heat intolerance, weight loss, increased sweating, along with a diffusely enlarged thyroid gland and exophthalmos, are indicative of Graves' disease, an autoimmune disorder causing hyperthyroidism.

Question 16: A 35-year-old man presents with hematuria, hypertension, and palpable bilateral flank masses. An ultrasound of the kidneys reveals multiple cysts in both kidneys. What is the most likely diagnosis?





Explanation: The most likely diagnosis is Autosomal dominant polycystic kidney disease (B). This condition is characterized by the presence of multiple cysts in both kidneys, hematuria, hypertension, and palpable flank masses, typically presenting in adulthood.

Question 17: A 28-year-old man presents with abdominal pain, diarrhea, and weight loss. Colonoscopy reveals transmural inflammation and skip lesions throughout the gastrointestinal tract. What is the most likely diagnosis?





Explanation: The most likely diagnosis is Crohn's disease (B). The presence of transmural inflammation and skip lesions (discontinuous areas of inflammation) throughout the gastrointestinal tract is characteristic of Crohn's disease.

Question 18: A 6-year-old boy is brought to the clinic due to a 2-day history of fever, sore throat, and a sandpaper-like rash. Physical examination reveals strawberry tongue and circumoral pallor. What is the most likely diagnosis?





Explanation: The most likely diagnosis is Scarlet fever (B). The combination of fever, sore throat, sandpaper-like rash, strawberry tongue, and circumoral pallor is characteristic of scarlet fever, which is caused by group A Streptococcus.

Question 19: A 45-year-old woman presents with easy bruising, fatigue, and frequent infections. Laboratory tests reveal pancytopenia and a bone marrow biopsy shows hypocellularity. What is the most likely diagnosis?





Explanation: The most likely diagnosis is Aplastic anemia (B). The presence of pancytopenia (reduction in all blood cell types) and a hypocellular bone marrow biopsy indicates aplastic anemia, a condition where the bone marrow fails to produce sufficient blood cells.

Question 20: A 30-year-old woman presents with multiple tender nodules on her shins and bilateral hilar lymphadenopathy on chest X-ray. She also reports recent fatigue and weight loss. What is the most likely diagnosis?





Explanation: The most likely diagnosis is Sarcoidosis (B). The presence of tender nodules on the shins (erythema nodosum), bilateral hilar lymphadenopathy on chest X-ray, along with fatigue and weight loss, is highly suggestive of sarcoidosis.
